Information

The Government Children's Family is managed by the Department of Social Services under the control of the Ministry of Social Welfare for the maintenance, education, training and rehabilitation of the orphaned children without parents or fatherless children.

Those involved in the implementation of the program:

Institutions Branch of Department of Social Services is responsible for the management of government child families. The Director (Institution) is headed by Additional Director, Deputy Director, Assistant Director, Social Service Officer at headquarters level and Custodian/Deputy Custodian at field level concerned with the management of government child families. The Deputy Director and Assistant Directors of the District Social Service Office supervise the field level institutions and coordinate between the field level and the headquarters. At the field level, a management committee led by the District Commissioner is the Children's Family Management Authority. The Caretaker/Deputy Caretaker of the concerned Government Child Family works as the Member-Secretary of the Committee. Besides, under the leadership of the Deputy Director, Director of Social Services, the Government Children's Family Admission Committee arranges the admission of children.

Services:

Fostering orphaned children up to 18 years of age Nurturing with love and care in a family environment Providing education and vocational training To achieve physical, intellectual and human excellence of the residents To provide employment to them with a view to rehabilitation and self-reliance

Service Recipient:

Orphans i.e. fatherless or fatherless poor children aged 6-9 years.

Service Center:

There are 42 boys, 42 girls and 1 mixed family located in the district headquarters and some upazilas, totaling 85 government child families. Download the list of families with children.

Service Delivery Procedures (In Brief):

Parents of 6-9 year old orphans will submit the application in the prescribed form. After preparing the draft list of applications received, the Supervisor/Deputy Supervisor convenes the meeting of the Admission Committee. Resident orphans are admitted and rehabilitated through maintenance, education and training up to the age of 18 years after selection by the resident and informing the applicant.

Functions:

In the month of December every year, the guardian submits an application to the concerned child's family or to the district social service office in the prescribed form; Verification of the age and health status of the applicant orphan through the Civil Surgeon or his representative; Final approval by the Admission Committee Free admission of orphans; Maintenance, education, training and rehabilitation of children into society with appropriate statusp

Relevant Acts/Rules/Policies:

1. Govt Shishu Sadan/Child Family Management Policy-2002
2. Children Act 2013
3. Children Act, 2013 (Amended 2018)

Areas of Citizen Participation:


Inform the authorities if any disturbance is observed in the management of the child family; Providing financial assistance with Zakat, Fitra, charity etc.; Any assistance in improving the quality of children's education; Assisting in the rehabilitation of the child financially, by providing employment or by providing information; Be kind to children;
Any form of assistance in the full development of the child.

Service Delivery Period:


1 month after receipt of application form for admission to child family Other services after the child is admitted till the child reaches 18 years of age
